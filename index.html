About Me

I am a senior at Edward R. Murrow High School with a interest in robotics, engineering, and technology. I enjoy working on hands-on engineering projects, especially those involving Arduino, electronics, and mechanical design. I am excited to continue exploring engineering in college and beyond.

My Project

I built a joystick-controlled robotic arm using Arduino and servo motors. The arm moves in the X and Y directions and can draw using a pen attached to the end. This project demonstrates robotic motion control and human–machine interaction.

Watch Process Video

Project Process

1. Designing

I used TinkerCAD to design the robotic arm layout, creating a detailed 3D model that allowed me to visualize each joint, servo placement, and range of motion. This digital prototype helped me plan the mechanical structure and test how the arm would move before building a physical version, ensuring that all components would fit together efficiently and function as intended.

2. Electronics

I used an Arduino UNO R3 as the main microcontroller to control the arm, along with a servomotor to provide precise movement. I integrated a joystick as the user interface, allowing me to manually control the arm’s motion. Before assembling the physical components, I thoroughly tested both the circuit and the code to ensure that the servo responded correctly to the joystick inputs, that the wiring was correct, and that the system performed smoothly without errors

3. Reflection

This project taught me valuable skills in servo control, Arduino programming, and circuit debugging, as well as the fundamentals of mechanical design. I gained hands-on experience in translating a concept into a working prototype and testing each component. This process strengthened my understanding of how hardware, software, and mechanical elements interact, and gave me practical experience in applying engineering principles to create functional, real-world devices.
